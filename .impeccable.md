## Design Context

### Users
Young adults (18–30), goal-oriented and busy. They move fast and often forget to slow down. Using Sonder in quiet moments — morning coffee before the day starts, or late at night reflecting on what happened. The app exists because its founders realized they had almost no memory of growing up, too focused on achievement to notice their actual lives.

### Brand Personality
**Intimate. Honest. Enduring.**

Not a wellness or meditation app. Not a productivity tool. A quiet companion for remembering who you actually are, day by day. The emotional register is: reflective, personal, a little serious — like the difference between a journal and a to-do list.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Literary editorial meets midnight journal.**

Dark theme — people write at night. Not the generic "dark mode SaaS" look with cyan accents and glowing cards. Think: the inside cover of a well-made book. Ink and paper translated to screen. Warm dark backgrounds (near-black with a faint warm tint), off-white type, restrained color.

Typography is the hero. A distinctive editorial serif for headlines — something that feels like a book title, not a startup. Clean, quiet sans for UI elements.

The glow in the background should feel like lamplight or candlelight — warm amber/brown tones, not purple/blue AI-gradient vibes.

**Anti-references**: No meditation app pastels. No SaaS dashboard blues. No glowing purple gradients. No "hustle culture" productivity aesthetic. No generic dark mode.

### Design Principles
1. **Restraint over decoration** — every element earns its place. No decorative elements without purpose.
2. **Typography does the heavy lifting** — the right font choice matters more than any visual effect.
3. **Warmth, not cold** — dark doesn't mean cold. Tint everything slightly warm.
4. **Intimate scale** — this is a personal product. The design should feel like it's talking to one person, not broadcasting to millions.
5. **Timeless over trendy** — this app is about memory and time. The design should still feel right in 5 years.
